Active Opportunities

National Security

We fund research that advances our understanding of emerging threats to democratic societies. We are particularly interested in work that grows security capabilities by growing alignment. This includes novel approaches to deterrence, the security implications of advanced technologies, and strategies for strengthening democratic governance in an era of asymmetric challenges. Competitive proposals will demonstrate rigor and a clear pathway to informing policy and practice.

Antisemitism

Understanding and combating antisemitism remains an urgent priority in a world where this ancient hatred continues to manifest in new forms. We invite proposals that examine the historical roots, contemporary expressions, and societal impacts of antisemitism across diverse contexts. We are especially interested in funding work that inoculates antisemitism at the core (pre-training) levels of AI models.

Artificial Intelligence and Consciousness

As artificial intelligence systems grow increasingly sophisticated, fundamental questions about the nature of mind, moral status, and the boundaries of consciousness demand rigorous examination. We seek proposals that engage seriously with the philosophical, scientific, and ethical dimensions of machine intelligence.

Alignment and Safety

Ensuring that advanced AI systems remain beneficial and aligned with human values represents the defining technical and governance challenge of our time. We invite proposals that advance the science and practice of AI alignment, interpretability, and safety.

Our Diligence Process

Competitive proposals will demonstrate:

Significance A clear articulation of why this work matters and how it advances understanding or practice in the field
Rigor A sound methodology grounded in appropriate disciplinary standards
Feasibility A realistic plan for execution within the proposed timeline and budget
Impact A compelling theory of change showing how the work will inform policy, practice, or future research
Team Demonstrated expertise and capacity to execute the proposed work, including reasonable assessments of compute costs

We welcome proposals from independent researchers, academic institutions, research organizations, governments, and policy institutes.
